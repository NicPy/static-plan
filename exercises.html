Train at home · 10 & 15 kg dumbbells

Build the core, burn the fat

3–4 sessions a week, ~30–40 minutes each. Full-body strength to protect muscle, focused core work to shape your midsection, and movement to widen the deficit.

🎯
Read this first: you cannot "spot reduce" belly fat. No amount of crunches burns fat off your stomach — fat leaves your whole body as the calorie deficit does its work. So this program isn't 100 abs a day. It's: (1) full-body strength to keep muscle and raise your metabolism, (2) direct core work to build and define the muscles under the fat, and (3) cardio + daily steps to burn more. The diet uncovers the abs; this training shapes them. More on the myth →

Steps matter more than you think. Sitting all day burns very little. A daily 30–40 min walk (or two 15-min walks after meals) can add 250–350 kcal of burn and helps digestion and cravings. This is the easiest "extra workout" you have.

Your weekly split

Do 3 days minimum (A · B · C). Add Day D if you have a 4th day. Leave at least one rest day between the two strength days.

Day A

Full-body strength + core

Goblet squat · RDL · one-arm row · shoulder press · then a core finisher (crunch + plank). Builds muscle, the metabolic engine.

Day B

Cardio / HIIT + core

15–20 min intervals (see below) · then obliques + lower-ab circuit. Burns calories, trains the waist.

Day C

Full-body strength + core

Reverse lunge · hip thrust · floor press · row · then carries + leg raises. Hits the other movements.

Day D · optional

Core circuit + steady cardio

Full core circuit (3 rounds) · then a 30–40 min brisk walk/run. Low-stress extra burn.

📅 Example week

  • Sun — Day A
  • Mon — walk + rest
  • Tue — Day B
  • Wed — walk + rest
  • Thu — Day C
  • Fri — rest
  • Sat — Day D (optional)

🔥 Warm-up (5 min)

Always, before lifting:

  • 1 min march in place / jog
  • 10 arm circles each way
  • 10 bodyweight squats
  • 10 hip hinges
  • 10 torso twists

🧊 Cool-down (3–5 min)

  • Hamstring stretch — 30s
  • Hip flexor stretch — 30s/side
  • Chest/doorway stretch — 30s
  • Cobra (ab stretch) — 30s
  • Slow breathing — 1 min
📈
Progress over 3 weeks (progressive overload): Week 1 — learn form, lighter end of reps, focus on control. Week 2 — add 1–2 reps per set, or a 4th set on big lifts. Week 3 — go heavier (15 kg where you used 10), or cut rest to ~45s to raise intensity. Always keep good form over more weight.

Day B — the cardio/HIIT block

15–20 minutes. 40 seconds hard, 20 seconds rest. Go through the list, then repeat 2–3 rounds.

Intervals (no equipment)

  • Mountain climbers
  • Jumping jacks
  • High knees
  • Squat-to-press (use 10 kg)
  • Burpees (or fast step-backs)

Prefer low-impact?

Swap the jumps for a brisk uphill walk or jog, stationary bike, or jump rope — 20–25 min at a pace where talking is hard. Easier on the knees, same fat-loss job. Finish with the core circuit below.

Core & abs

Weighted Crunch

10 kg on chest
3 × 12–15Upper abs
  • Hold the dumbbell on your chest; curl shoulders up, don't yank your neck.
  • Crunch the ribs toward hips; pause 1s at the top.
  • Week 3: slow the lowering to 3 seconds.

Lying Leg Raise

Bodyweight
3 × 10–15Lower abs
  • Lie flat, hands under your lower back. Raise straight legs to vertical.
  • Lower slowly; stop before your back arches off the floor.
  • Too hard? Bend the knees. Too easy? Pause 2s at the bottom.

Bicycle Crunch

Bodyweight
3 × 20 (10/side)Obliques + abs
  • Opposite elbow toward opposite knee; extend the other leg.
  • Rotate from the ribs, slow and controlled — not fast and floppy.
  • Keep your lower back pressed down.

Weighted Russian Twist

10 kg
3 × 16 (8/side)Obliques
  • Lean back ~45°, heels light or lifted. Rotate the weight side to side.
  • Move from the waist, not just the arms; tap the floor each side.
  • Keep your chest tall — don't hunch.

Plank

Bodyweight
3 × 30–60 sDeep core
  • Forearms down, body in one straight line — no sagging hips, no peak.
  • Squeeze glutes and brace abs as if about to be punched.
  • Progress by adding 10s each week.

Side Plank

Bodyweight
3 × 20–40 s/sideObliques
  • Elbow under shoulder, hips stacked and lifted high.
  • Straight line head to feet; don't let the hip drop.
  • Easier: drop the bottom knee to the floor.

Dead Bug

Bodyweight
3 × 8/sideDeep core control
  • On your back, arms up, knees at 90°. Lower opposite arm & leg slowly.
  • Keep your lower back glued to the floor the whole time.
  • The best move for learning to brace — foundation for everything.

3 × 10/sideObliques

Full-body strength

Your core braces on every one of these — that's why compound lifts build a strong, tight midsection and protect muscle while you cut.

Goblet Squat

15 kg at chest
3–4 × 10–12Legs · glutes · core
  • Hold the dumbbell vertically at your chest. Sit down between your hips.
  • Knees track over toes; chest up; go to thighs-parallel or lower.
  • Drive through the heels to stand.
▶ Watch demo

Romanian Deadlift

15 kg (+10 kg)
3 × 10–12Hamstrings · glutes · back
  • Soft knees; push hips back, dumbbells sliding down your thighs.
  • Feel a stretch in the hamstrings, then squeeze glutes to stand.
  • Flat back the whole way — never round.
▶ Watch demo

One-Arm Row

15 kg / side
3 × 10/sideBack · lats · biceps
  • Hand & knee on a chair/bed, flat back. Pull the dumbbell to your hip.
  • Lead with the elbow; squeeze the shoulder blade.
  • Don't twist your torso to heave it up.
▶ Watch demo

Floor Press

10–15 kg / hand
3 × 10–12Chest · shoulders · triceps
  • Lie on the floor, dumbbells over chest. Lower until elbows lightly touch down.
  • Press back up under control. Floor protects your shoulders.
  • One dumbbell? Do it single-arm, 3 × 10/side.
▶ Watch demo

Standing Shoulder Press

10 kg / hand
3 × 8–12Shoulders · triceps · core
  • Dumbbells at shoulders; press overhead without arching your back.
  • Brace your abs hard — standing presses are a sneaky core move.
  • Lower with control to ear height.
▶ Watch demo

Reverse Lunge

10 kg / hand
3 × 8/legLegs · glutes · balance
  • Step back, drop the back knee toward the floor, front shin vertical.
  • Push through the front heel to return. Easier on the knees than forward lunges.
  • Keep your torso tall and core braced.

Dumbbell Hip Thrust

15 kg on hips
3 × 12–15Glutes · hamstrings
  • Upper back on a couch/bed edge, dumbbell across the hips.
  • Drive hips up to a straight line; squeeze glutes hard at the top.
  • Strong glutes improve posture and pull in your lower belly.

Put it together — copy these sessions

Day A · strength + core
  1. Warm-up (5 min)
  2. Goblet Squat — 3×10–12
  3. One-Arm Row — 3×10/side
  4. Shoulder Press — 3×8–12
  5. Weighted Crunch — 3×12–15
  6. Plank — 3×30–60s
  7. Cool-down
Day B · cardio + core
  1. Warm-up (5 min)
  2. HIIT intervals — 2–3 rounds (15–20 min)
  3. Bicycle Crunch — 3×20
  4. Russian Twist — 3×16
  5. Side Plank — 3×20–40s/side
  6. Cool-down
Day C · strength + core
  1. Warm-up (5 min)
  2. Reverse Lunge — 3×8/leg
  3. Hip Thrust — 3×12–15
  4. Floor Press — 3×10–12
  5. Suitcase Carry — 3×30–40m/side
  6. Leg Raise — 3×10–15
  7. Cool-down
Day D · optional core + walk
  1. Warm-up (5 min)
  2. Core circuit ×3: Crunch 15 · Leg Raise 12 · Russian Twist 16 · Plank 40s · Mountain Climbers 40s
  3. 30–40 min brisk walk or easy jog
⚠️
Form & safety: never train through sharp pain. If a weight breaks your form, drop to a lighter one or fewer reps. Sore-but-functional is normal; joint pain is not. With a rapid calorie cut you may feel a bit weaker — that's expected, just keep the protein high.
