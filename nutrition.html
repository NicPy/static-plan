The theory · 8-minute read

Why food — not crunches — loses fat

You don't need to memorise this. Read it once and the food plan will feel obvious instead of like rules to obey.

The one-paragraph version

Your body runs on energy measured in calories. Eat fewer than you burn and your body makes up the difference by burning stored fat — that's a calorie deficit, the only thing that loses fat. Protein keeps you full and protects muscle while you do it. Fiber (from veg, fruit, whole grains, legumes) keeps you full on fewer calories. Sugar, refined carbs and alcohol add calories fast without filling you up. Everything below is just detail on that sentence.

1 · Calories & the deficit

A calorie is a unit of energy. You burn calories just being alive (breathing, organs, brain) plus everything you do. Your daily burn is your TDEE. Here's a worked example for a mostly-seated day:

The math, simply: a 500 kcal daily deficit × 7 days = 3,500 kcal ≈ 0.45 kg of fat a week. Add the workouts and daily steps and you nudge toward 0.6–0.75 kg. Over 3 weeks that's ~2–2.5 kg of real fat, plus water weight that drops fast in week one.
⛔
Don't go too low. Eating far below 1,500 kcal backfires: you lose muscle, energy and sleep, and you rebound. A moderate deficit you can actually sustain beats a brutal one you quit on day 9. See the realistic target →

2 · Your three macros

Calories come from three nutrients — protein, carbs, fat. Here's how the ~1,650 kcal target splits:

Protein 41% Carbs 31% Fat 28%
170 g protein · 680 kcal 130 g carbs · 520 kcal 50 g fat · 450 kcal

1 g protein = 4 kcal · 1 g carb = 4 kcal · 1 g fat = 9 kcal · 1 g alcohol = 7 kcal.

3 · Protein — the non-negotiable

Protein is the building block of muscle. In a deficit your body looks for tissue to break down for energy — eat enough protein and it spares your muscle and burns fat instead. Bonus: protein is the most filling macro and takes the most energy to digest.

Your target: ~170 g/day (about 2 g per kg of bodyweight) — split across meals, ~30–45 g each. That's the single most important habit on this plan after the calorie target.

Easy protein in Israel

  • Chicken / turkey breast — ~31 g per 100 g cooked
  • Cottage cheese 5% — ~11 g per 100 g
  • Eggs — ~6.5 g each
  • Greek yogurt / gvina levana — ~10 g per 100 g
  • Tuna, salmon, tilapia (amnoon)
  • Lentils, chickpeas, white beans, tofu
✅
Do this: build every meal around a protein first, then add veg, then a small carb. If you only nail one thing each day, nail protein.

4 · Carbs — not the enemy

Carbs are your body's preferred fuel — they power your workouts and your brain. You don't need to cut them to zero; you need to choose the right ones and the right amount.

Prefer — complex

Slow & filling

Oats, whole-wheat pita/bread, bulgur, freekeh, quinoa, brown rice, sweet potato, legumes, fruit and all vegetables. Digest slowly, packed with fiber, keep you full and steady.

Limit — refined

Fast & empty

White bread, sugary pastries (burekas, rugelach), soft drinks, candy, fruit juice. Spike then crash your energy, easy to overeat, little fiber.

📉
Glycemic index, in one line: low-GI foods raise blood sugar slowly and keep you full longer. Pairing carbs with protein/fat/fiber lowers the spike — which is exactly how the meals here are built.

5 · Fat — needed, just measured

Fat isn't fattening by itself — but it's calorie-dense (9 kcal/g), so small amounts add up fast. You need some for hormones and to absorb vitamins. Aim ~50 g/day from good sources.

Good fats

Olive oil, tahini, avocado, nuts & seeds, olives, fatty fish (salmon, sardines). The backbone of the Mediterranean diet.

Watch the dose

Even good fats are easy to overpour. A tablespoon of olive oil or tahini is ~120 kcal. Measure it — don't free-pour over salad.

6 · Fiber, sugar & alcohol

Fiber (vegetables, fruit with skin, legumes, whole grains) adds bulk and slows digestion, so you feel full on fewer calories and stay regular. Aim for vegetables at lunch and dinner. This is the easiest way to not feel hungry on a deficit.

Sugar isn't poison, but it's calorie-dense and doesn't fill you up — especially when you drink it. A glass of juice or a soft drink can be 120–150 kcal that your body barely notices. Swap to water, soda water, unsweetened iced coffee or tea. This one swap alone can be a chunk of your daily deficit.

Alcohol is 7 kcal/g — almost as dense as fat — with zero nutrition, and it pauses fat-burning while your body processes it. A 500 ml beer is ~200 kcal; two and you've erased a day's deficit, plus it loosens your food choices. For these 3 weeks, keep it to a rare single drink, or skip it. If you do drink, make it your "carb" for that meal.

Trick: compare two products by their per-100 g calories and protein, not the marketing on the front. "Light" / "דל" doesn't always mean fewer calories.

8 · Why the scale jumps around

Your weight can swing 1–2 kg in a day with zero change in fat. It's water — driven by salt, carbs (each gram of stored carb holds ~3 g water), stress, sleep, and for context a big salty meal can show +1 kg the next morning that's gone in two days.

📅
So: weigh under the same conditions (morning, after the toilet, before eating) and judge the trend over a week, not day-to-day noise. The Tracker draws that trend line for you.
🎯
The belly-fat myth, settled.

There is no "spot reduction." You cannot burn fat from a specific area by training it. Belly fat disappears as your total body fat drops — driven by the calorie deficit. Ab exercises strengthen and build the muscle under the fat (so it looks defined once revealed) and burn some calories, but the flat stomach is made in the kitchen.
