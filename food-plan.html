Your 3-week food plan

Three meals plus one snack a day, built from in-season Israeli food you actually like to eat. Every meal shows a portion, calories and an easy swap — so you're never stuck.

Daily target

Each day lands around 1,600–1,700 kcal and 150–170 g protein.

~1,650 kcal 170 g protein 130 g carbs 50 g fat 3 L water
🛒 Matching shopping list
🥩
Protein & veg first. Eat those before the carbs at every meal — you'll fill up and naturally eat less.
🔁
Swap freely. Use the “alt:” option, or trade any meal for another in the same column — they're matched in calories & protein.
💧
Summer hydration. 3 L water/day. Thirst masquerades as hunger in the heat — drink a glass before snacking.

🕯️ Shabbat & family meals

You don't have to be the weird one at the table. Strategy: fill half your plate with salad and veg first, take one fist-size portion of the starch (challah, rice, potatoes — pick one, not all three), a palm or two of protein, and skip seconds. Have the fruit, skip the cake — or take a few bites and stop. One relaxed meal won't undo a week; a whole day of grazing will.

🍽️ Eating out / hummusiya

Grilled meat or fish + salads is your friend — most Israeli grills do this perfectly. At a hummusiya, share one hummus with a friend and load up on the salad and a single pita rather than a whole bowl each. Ask for dressing/tahini on the side. Water or soda, not a soft drink or beer. Free foods & drinks (eat/drink freely)

When you're hungry between meals, these barely cost calories and keep you full:

  • Cucumber
  • Tomato
  • Pepper
  • Lettuce / leaves
  • Celery
  • Carrot
  • Radish
  • Pickles
  • Lemon
  • Herbs
  • Water
  • Soda water
  • Black coffee
  • Unsweetened tea
  • Iced coffee (no sugar)

Coffee is fine (1–3/day) — just watch what goes in it. A daily diet soft drink is OK if it stops you reaching for sugar.
