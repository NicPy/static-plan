The stuff that makes or breaks it

Tips & FAQ

The plan works on paper. These are the real-life levers — sleep, stress, social meals and how to recover from a bad day — that decide whether it works for you.

Five habits that matter most

1

Protein at every meal

Hit ~170 g/day. It's the difference between losing fat and losing muscle.

2

Walk daily

8–10k steps. Two 15-min walks after meals beats one heroic gym session you skip.

3

Sleep 7–8 h

Poor sleep raises hunger hormones and cravings. It's an underrated fat-loss tool.

4

Drink water first

3 L/day. A glass before meals and snacks curbs false hunger, especially in the heat.

5

Plan the hard moments

Decide before Shabbat / eating out what you'll do. Willpower fails; a plan doesn't.

First, don't panic — weight stalls for days are normal, usually water (salt, carbs, stress, poor sleep, or muscle soreness holding fluid). Check the weekly trend and your waist measurement before changing anything. If you're genuinely flat for 7+ days: tighten portion accuracy (those free-poured oils and "bites" add up), make sure you're truly hitting protein, add 1–2k steps, and prioritise sleep. Don't slash calories below ~1,500 — that backfires.

One bad meal or day will not undo your progress — it takes a big, sustained surplus to gain real fat, just like it takes a sustained deficit to lose it. The mistake isn't the slip; it's the "I've ruined it, might as well write off the week" spiral. Don't compensate by starving the next day. Just get back to the very next meal as normal. Consistency over 3 weeks beats perfection over 3 days.

For the best 3-week result, keep it rare. Alcohol is 7 kcal/g, pauses fat-burning, wrecks sleep quality, and loosens your food decisions. If there's an occasion, cap it at one or two drinks, count them as that meal's "carbs," and choose lower-calorie options (dry wine, spirit + soda) over beer and cocktails. Then back to water.
